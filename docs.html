Laws of Motion

The institutional surface for how Yinspire measures physics, intent, and fairness. Not decoration — retrieval, audit, and ship criteria share one vocabulary.

Manifesto

Motion is interface. When stiffness, damping, and mass are left implicit, products drift into inconsistency. Yinspire treats every transition as a governed simulation: bounded energy, perceptible cause, and a measurable obligation to the person on the other side of the glass.

This document is the canonical reference for tokens surfaced in env, the Search Matrix, and Justice audits. Numbers below are normative for the “Final” lane unless a team charter explicitly overrides them.

Laws of motion

I. Conservation of intent

A gesture must not borrow kinetic language it cannot pay for. Spring language requires restorable equilibrium; inertial language requires deceleration you can read; magnetic language requires an attraction field you can predict before the snap.

II. Observable cause

Every state change names its driver: user impulse, system resolution, or cross-surface handoff. If the driver cannot be named, the motion is not ship-ready.

III. Justice as latency bound

“Fair” is not a vibe. It is a bound on how long truth is withheld from the user — how long the surface pretends confidence while physics or data are still unresolved.

Stiffness · 180

k = 180 (with canonical mass and damping in the default bundle) is the gold standard for institutional spring surfaces: sheets, modals, and navigation chrome that must read as calm but decisive.

  • Below ~120 — comfortable for large amplitude or educational motion, but risks “mush” on dense chrome.
  • 180 — restores to equilibrium within a window that matches reading rhythm on modern displays without overshoot chatter when paired with nominal damping.
  • Above ~260 — reserved for small targets, tool transitions, or explicit “snap” families where the affordance is precision, not drift.

Teams may fork profiles (for example the sheet family at k = 320), but the default token in Standardization remains 180 / ζ_nominal / m_nominal so cross-surface muscle memory holds across products.

Justice score

The Justice Score is a unitary index from 0–100 aggregating measurable signals from the motion trace and its relationship to declared intent. It is designed for retrieval (Search Matrix) and gatekeeping (ship lanes), not vanity metrics.

Composition (illustrative weights for the shell)

J = 100 × ( 0.34·S_sym + 0.28·S_cause + 0.22·S_bound + 0.16·S_handoff )

  • S_sym — symmetry of acceleration phases around the declared rest state (no hidden torque).
  • S_cause — alignment between input timing and visible response onset (no anticipatory lying).
  • S_bound — energy and overshoot remain inside the envelope implied by stiffness and damping class.
  • S_handoff — continuity across route or layer changes; handoff shocks lower the score.

Thresholds such as “J ≥ 90” in the Search Matrix map to high-confidence retrieval: surfaces that are unlikely to surprise under audit replay.

Justice audit

Audits replay transitions under fixed device profiles, logging phase traces and comparing them to the declared law + token bundle. Failures are typed (symmetry, cause, bound, handoff) so Activity notifications route to the right owners.

The Activity hub surfaces audit state transitions: queued, in review, passed, or policy-blocked. Team mentions and Series updates share the same transport so retention is not siloed by notification category.
